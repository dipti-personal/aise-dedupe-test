Page C — Roses and Daisies

Overview

Roses and daisies are among the most widely recognized flowering plants in the world. They are studied extensively in botany, horticulture, and cultural history. Roses are valued for their fragrance, structural complexity, and symbolic associations, while daisies are admired for their resilience, adaptability, and simple morphology. Together, these flowers serve as excellent examples of how plant species evolve different survival strategies and aesthetic traits across ecosystems.

Roses

Roses are known for their layered petals and strong fragrance. They are commonly used in gardens, perfumes, and symbolic events. Across cultures, roses have been used to represent love, secrecy, and diplomacy. Different varieties of roses are cultivated worldwide, each adapted to specific climates and horticultural practices. Their historical significance spans ancient civilizations, religious symbolism, and modern commercial floriculture.

Daisies

Daisies represent simplicity and resilience. They grow easily in many climates and are often associated with purity and innocence.
